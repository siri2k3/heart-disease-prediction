ABSTRACT

Heart stroke is a leading cause of death and disability worldwide. Early diagnosis and prevention are crucial for reducing the burden of heart stroke. Machine learning has emerged as a powerful tool for heart stroke prediction, but its effectiveness relies heavily on the availability of large amounts of labeled data. Traditional supervised learning methods often face challenges due to the limited availability of labeled data and the high cost of manual labeling. Semi-supervised machine learning offers a promising solution to this challenge by leveraging both clinical and non clinical data to improve prediction performance.
